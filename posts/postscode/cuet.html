Why Documents Matter More Than Your Score

Your CUET score gets you a seat offer. Your documents confirm you actually get that seat.

Missing documents can lead to rejection of a seat — even after it has been allotted to you. Universities verify your eligibility, category, and identity before confirming admission. If anything is missing or mismatched, the next candidate on the merit list moves in.

For CUET UG 2026, a staggering 15,68,866 students registered for the examination — making competition fiercer than ever. With that many qualified candidates waiting in the wings, there is zero room for a documentation slip-up.

CUET Counselling Documents Required 2026: The Master Checklist

1. Academic Documents

These form the foundation of your application. Carry originals as well as self-attested photocopies for all of them.

  • Class 10 Mark Sheet – Original + photocopy (proof of date of birth and board)
  • Class 12 Mark Sheet – Original + photocopy (primary eligibility proof)
  • Class 12 Passing / Qualifying Certificate – Different from your mark sheet; issued by your board separately
  • Degree Semester Mark Sheets (for PG applicants) – All semesters, original + photocopy
  • CUET 2026 Score Card – Download from the official NTA portal and print a clear copy
  • CUET 2026 Admit Card – A colour printout; some universities treat it as identity proof during verification

You must carry the original and photocopy of 10th, 12th, and all semesters of the degree course (if applicable), along with your CUET Scorecard.

2. Identity & Address Proof

Universities require at least one government-issued photo ID. Acceptable documents include:

  • Aadhaar Card (most widely accepted)
  • Passport
  • Voter ID Card
  • PAN Card
  • Driver's Licence

A copy of a valid ID proof — such as an Aadhaar Card, Aadhaar Enrollment Receipt, PAN Card, or Voter ID Card — is mandatory during the admission process.

Carry the original along with a photocopy. Many counters scan your Aadhaar directly, but it is safer to have a physical copy ready.

3. Transfer & Migration Certificates

These documents are often overlooked because students don't need them for exam registration — but they become essential at admission time.

  • Transfer Certificate (TC) – Issued by your Class 12 school; confirms you have left the institution
  • Migration Certificate – Required if you studied under a board or university different from the one where you're seeking admission; issued by your previous institution or board
  • Character / Conduct Certificate – Issued by your school principal or last attended institution

A migration certificate — both the original and a photocopy — is required if applicable. If you're a student from a CBSE or state board applying to a central university in a different state, you will almost certainly need this.

4. Category & Reservation Certificates

If you belong to a reserved category, these documents determine whether you receive the benefit of reservation during seat allotment.

  • SC / ST Certificate – Issued by your district's Revenue / Social Welfare Department
  • OBC (Non-Creamy Layer) Certificate – Must be current (issued within the financial year, in most cases)
  • EWS Certificate – Economically Weaker Section certificate; renewed annually
  • PwD Certificate – Disability certificate from a government hospital or recognised medical board
  • Kashmiri Migrant Certificate – If applicable

The important reservation-related documents include caste certificate and reservation category certificate, which must be submitted alongside academic documents.

Important: OBC-NCL and EWS certificates expire at the end of each financial year. If your certificate was issued before April 1, 2026, check whether the university requires a freshly issued one.

5. Photographs and Signature

  • Passport-Size Photographs – Carry at least 5 to 8 recent (within the last 3 months) colour photographs on a white background
  • Signature on White Paper – Some universities collect an ink signature during reporting

Photographs and signatures must be recent passport-size photos — a minimum of 5 in number.

6. Documents for Foreign / NRI Applicants

If you belong to the NRI, PIO, OCI, SAARC, Africa, ASEAN, or Other Foreign National categories, you must submit a PIO or OCI card as applicable, along with a Medical Fitness Certificate from any recognised medical practitioner certified by the Medical Council of India.

Step-by-Step: The Document Verification Process

Understanding how verification works helps you prepare better.

The counselling process comprises the following stages: registering on the university's website, choice filling, seat allotment, acceptance of the allotted seat, fee payment, and reporting to the college for document verification.

Here is what typically happens on verification day:

  1. Arrive with a complete folder – Organise documents in the order listed by the university's official checklist (always download this from the specific university's website)
  2. Present originals first – Officials will physically inspect originals before accepting photocopies
  3. Submit self-attested photocopies – Sign across each photocopy with the note "Self-Attested" and today's date
  4. Biometric / Photo Verification – Many universities now capture biometrics at this stage
  5. Pay the admission fee – Usually online, sometimes at the counter; do this only after documents are accepted
  6. Collect your allotment / admission letter – This is your official proof of enrollment

Documents must be carried in original — furnishing photocopies alone will not be accepted. Candidates should also ensure that the documents are valid and issued by relevant authorities.

University-Specific Requirements: What You Must Check

This is where many students make a critical mistake: they prepare a generic checklist without reading their specific university's requirements.

The required documents may vary from university to university.

For example:

  • Delhi University (DU) has its own structured CSAS portal and may require additional certificates for specific programmes
  • BHU (Banaras Hindu University) follows its own schedule and may have programme-specific eligibility documents
  • JNU may require research proposals or writing samples for certain postgraduate programmes

Top universities such as Delhi University, Banaras Hindu University, Dr B.R. Ambedkar University, and Jawaharlal Nehru University publish counselling dates on their official websites. Always check the official admissions page of each university you've applied to.

Common Mistakes That Cost Students Their Seats

Even well-prepared students make these avoidable errors:

  • Carrying expired category certificates – Especially OBC-NCL and EWS certificates that expired on March 31
  • Mismatched names – Name on Aadhaar doesn't match Class 10 certificate; get an affidavit ready if this applies to you
  • Missing migration certificate – Overlooked because it's not needed for the exam itself
  • Black-and-white photographs – Many universities require colour photos only
  • Unsigned photocopies – Self-attestation is not optional; unsigned copies are routinely rejected
  • Wrong format of TC – Transfer certificates must carry the school's official seal and principal's signature

Spot Counselling: A Second Chance — If You're Ready

Those who would not be allotted a seat in the original counselling rounds can participate in spot counselling by registering on the official websites of the participating institutes. Such candidates will have to participate by visiting the institutes and getting the documents verified by the institutes' admission officials.

Spot counselling fills seats that remain vacant after regular rounds. The timeline is tight — often just a day or two — which means your document folder must already be complete and ready before the spot round is even announced.

FAQ: CUET Counselling Documents 2026

Q1. Is the CUET scorecard enough to prove my eligibility during counselling?
No. The CUET scorecard confirms your test performance but not your academic eligibility. You must also submit Class 10 and Class 12 mark sheets, your qualifying certificate, and other documents listed by your university.

Q2. How many photocopies of each document should I carry?
Carry at least 3 to 4 self-attested photocopies of each document. Different counters (document verification, fee payment, ID check) may each retain a copy.

Q3. What if my name is spelled differently on my Aadhaar and marksheet?
This is a common issue. Prepare a notarised affidavit explaining the discrepancy, along with both documents. Inform the admission officer upfront rather than waiting for them to flag it.

Q4. Do I need a fresh OBC-NCL certificate for CUET 2026 counselling?
Most central universities require an OBC-NCL certificate issued in or after April 2026 (i.e., for the current financial year 2026–27). Certificates from 2025–26 are typically not accepted. Check the specific university's notification to be sure.

Q5. Can I submit digital copies of documents during online counselling?
For online counselling rounds, scanned copies are generally accepted for initial verification. However, you will still need to present original documents when you physically report to the institution for final enrollment.
